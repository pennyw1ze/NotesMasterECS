\begin{abstract}
\label{abstract}
In 2026, member states of the European Union are expected to have running
their own EUDI wallet applications instances compliant withe the ARF
architecture guidelines. The goal of the ARF architecture is to provide privacy
preserving anonymous credentials to users, but the current implementation fails.

In this thesis we will explore the current state of the art and give an overview
of current implementations of anonymous credentials. We will then explore the
post-quantum settings which has not been faced by the current implementations
and we will give an important result which allowded us to implememnt efficient
privacy preserving post-quantum anonymous credentials, preserving legacy architectures
in their almost entirety.
\end{abstract}
